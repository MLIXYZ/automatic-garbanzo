Bay Area's Climate Crisis

Understand the causes, impacts, and solutions to the most pressing environmental challenges of our time.

Impact

Our Park

The Golden Gate National Recreation Area is facing a challenging future as climate change brings rising sea levels, bigger storms, and more frequent droughts and wildfires. These changes are putting incredible pressure on this national recreation area's fragile coastal habitats and the wildlife.

Image credit: Santiago Mejia, The Chronicle

Our Seas

Rising sea levels and warmer oceans are changing our coastline, increasing flooding and damaging marine habitats and local seafood populations, threatening the region's fisheries and coastal ecosystems.

Image credit: KQED

Wildfires

High and dry weather during the recent drought worsened low precipitation, which fuels record-breaking wildfires—eight of California's 20 largest have occurred since 2017, including the August Complex Fire which is one of the largest in state history.

Image credit: Olga R. Rodriguez, Associated Press

Snow

As the temperature rises, the snow conditions change. For example, higher temperatures will cause less powdery snow and instead you will have dense snow which is more dangerous.

Image credit: Brian Walker, Sierra-at-Tahoe

Storm

As the climate warms, California is expected to face stronger and more frequent heavy rainstorms caused by intense atmospheric rivers, leading to severe flooding and landslides. These extreme wet periods, followed by longer dry spells, will make water management more difficult and increase risks to communities, infrastructure, and the environment.

Image credit: UCLA

Health Impacts

In San Francisco, some neighborhoods face far greater risks from extreme heat and smoke because of factors like fewer trees, more pavement, and higher pollution.

Image credit: Jae C. Hong, Associated Press

Solution

Public Transit

According to the Bay Area BART website, without BART there would be 2,000-4,000 more cars on the road daily, and it would be extremely challenging to offset the pollution.

Image credit: Jason O'Rear, AIA New York

Electric Vehicles

California proposed a large incentive to purchase and own electric vehicles which will cut down emissions and stop polluting our cities, and by 2035 California will stop selling gasoline vehicles.

Image credit: Lucidmotors.com

CA 2045 Carbon Neutrality

California has proposed a plan to be carbon neutral by 2045 and to cut air pollution by 71% and slash 85% of greenhouse gas emissions by 2045 while reducing fossil fuel consumption.

Image credit: f11photo – stock.adobe.com
